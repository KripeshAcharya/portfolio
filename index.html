About

My Name is Kripesh Acharya.

I am a graduate student pursuing a Master of Science in Cybersecurity at Mercy University in Dobbs Ferry, New York, with an expected graduation in December 2024. Committed to continuous learning, I strive to identify and address security gaps within the cybersecurity field. I hold a Bachelor of Science in Computing with First Class Honors and possess a range of certifications, including ISC2 Certified in Cybersecurity, Security Blue Team Junior Security Analyst Pathway, Cisco Junior Cybersecurity Analyst Career Path, NIST Risk Management Framework, and ICSI CNSS Certified Network Security Specialist.

My experience includes leading major projects such as the Efficacy of LLM Powered Malware Obfuscation (v1) with MITRE, investigating Compounding Threat Trends on National Critical Infrastructure (v1) with Sandia National Laboratories, and developing Scanning the Matrix: Harnessing Automation for Vulnerability Scans (v1) with the National Security Agency.

In addition to these major projects, I have completed various technical initiatives such as configuring Snort and creating detection rules for network traffic and attack patterns, developing comprehensive red team reports, utilizing Raspberry Pi for sensor integrations, delivering cybersecurity awareness training, and configuring firewalls and intrusion detection systems. I have also engaged in vulnerability management, threat analysis, and leveraging open-source intelligence tools and network analysis to strengthen security postures.

With two years of experience as a Technical Support Executive at an Internet Service Provider, I have also honed my ability to troubleshoot and resolve complex technical issues, further enriching my practical understanding of network and systems security.
